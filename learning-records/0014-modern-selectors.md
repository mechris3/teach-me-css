# Modern selectors understood

The user completed Lesson 13 covering: :has() (parent/conditional selector with combinators and form states), :is() (selector list shorthand with specificity of most-specific argument), :where() (zero-specificity version of :is()), :not() (exclusion with complex selectors), combining them, and the specificity rules for each.

## Implications
- Selector toolkit is now comprehensive: combinators, pseudo-classes, functional selectors, specificity control.
- Combined with @layer, :where() gives the user a complete specificity management strategy.
- Ready for: @scope, view transitions, logical properties, or the full cascade algorithm.
